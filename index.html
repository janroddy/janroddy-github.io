research.

Python Modules: Interdisciplinary K-12 Computer Science Education

My research is advised by Dr. Yolanda Reimer, as part of a larger coalition of work to broaden participation in CS in Montana High Schools. In collaboration with K-12 teachers across the MT, we are working to integrate Python programming exercises into various subjects from mathematics, natural sciences, the arts, social studies, and business. I love delving into a myriad of subjects and writing the CS exercises, but my favorite part of the project is working with incredible students and teachers.

By covering Python programming in a class’s existing curriculum, students can:
  • interactively reinforce important concepts;
  • engage with difficult concepts in a new way;
  • gain exposure to how computing can be used to solve problems in various fields;
  • discover the relevancy of computing to their interests and strengths;
