Python Modules: Interdisciplinary K-12 Computer Science Education

Most Montana High School students are never exposed to Computer Science (CS) Education and lack understanding of the CS field. Programming is a foundational skill for nearly every profession and learning CS teaches students critical reasoning and problem-solving skills. To address this problem, in collaboration with K-12 teachers across the state, we are working to integrate Python programming modules into various subjects from mathematics, natural sciences, the arts, social studies, and business. By covering computational thinking in a class’s existing curriculum, students can interactively reinforce important concepts and gain exposure to how computing can be used to solve problems in various fields. Through the cross-disciplinary Python modules, students can engage with difficult concepts in a new way and discover the relevancy of computing to their interests and strengths.
